1. Denali National Park and Preserve

This national park is located in Alaska. It's largest mountain is actually called Denali mountain. It has extremely diverse wildlife with 39 species of mammals, 169 bird species, and 1 amphibian species specially adapted to cold. I am especially interested in the Dall Sheep! Here is a link if you want to learn more about it!

2. The Smithsonian Museum

The Smithsonian is the world's largest museum with 19 museums and the national zoo (I want to visit the Air and Space Meuseum the most). It is located in Washington, DC. Here is the meuseum website for more information!

3. Grand Cayman

Grand Cayman is an island with amazing coral reefs and mangrove forests that you can explore in many different ways. It is also home to the Ambassadors of the environment program. To learn more about the wildlife or the Ambassadors program here

4. Monteray Bay Aquarium

Monteray Bay Aquarium is located in California. It has a large variety of animals and you can even watch some of them live through their website. It also has many cool teen programs. For more info and the live videos, click here.

5. Evergreen, Colordao

Evergreen is a great place to go do fun outdoor sports, also to take in the natural beauty. It is great for skiing, kyaking and rafting, ice skating, and all sorts of other fun activities. Here is a website where you can learn more.

6. Zambezi River

The Zambezi River is a river located in Africa that eventually leads to Victoria Falls. Elephants will sometimes cross it looking for a safe spot from predators and heat. Sometimes this river is flowing heavily and sometimes not very much at all. Here is a link with more infomation.
